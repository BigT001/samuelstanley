I spent three hours last night wrestling with Docker configurations and a broken GitHub Action that decided to fail for absolutely no logical reason. If you have ever stared at a terminal at 2 AM while your generator hums in the background, you know that DevOps is often where developer joy goes to die.

Which is why the news of Vercel acquiring Egypt-based Stakpak hit me right in the gut.

The startup, founded by George Fahmy and launched just in 2023, built an open-source autonomous DevOps agent. Think of it as an AI companion that handles the annoying infrastructure boilerplate we all hate. Before this acquisition, they raised a modest 500K USD pre-seed in early 2025. Now, their team is joining Vercel.

This is not an isolated event. Vercel recently bought Better Auth, another brilliant utility tool with deep African roots founded by an Ethiopian developer. Guillermo Rauch and his team are clearly scanning the open-source ecosystem, finding tools that make developer workflows seamless, and snapping them up.

![Writing code late at night](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1517694712282-14f494bc6f0e?q=80&w=1200&auto=format&fit=crop)

### Why Open-Source is the Ultimate Hack for African Devs

For a long time, the dominant narrative for tech founders in Nigeria was simple: build a fintech app, struggle with licensing, fight with legacy banking infrastructure, and burn through capital trying to acquire retail users who are already battling Sapa. 

But there is a different breed of builders now. Whether you are running on a clean solar setup in a quiet corner of Jos, sharing bandwidth in a noisy Gbagada co-working space, or coding with the grid-toggle anxiety in Akure, the internet has leveled the playing field for developer tooling.

When you build open-source, your market is global from day one. You do not need an expensive sales team in San Francisco. If your tool solves a real bottleneck—like Stakpak did by automating messy infrastructure setups—developers will find it. They will star it on GitHub. They will use it in their side projects. And eventually, the big cloud platforms will notice.

![Analyzing performance graphs and system data](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1551288049-bebda4e38f71?q=80&w=1200&auto=format&fit=crop)

### The Shift from "Enterprise Sales" to "Dev-First"

Let us talk about the execution. Stakpak did not win by writing fifty-page corporate pitch decks. They won because they built a tool that solved a high-friction problem: infrastructure management.

Historically, setting up staging environments, managing Kubernetes, and configuring continuous deployment has required serious engineering hours. Stakpak’s AI agent practically says, "Let me write and manage those YAML files while you focus on the actual application logic."

For small teams, this is a massive win. If you are a lean team trying to ship a fast MVP without the budget for a dedicated DevOps engineer, an autonomous agent keeping your pipeline intact is a lifesaver. Vercel knows this. By bringing Stakpak under its wing, they are cementing their position as the default platform where developers go to deploy literally anything without thinking about the underlying servers.

### No Gree for Anybody, Just Build

This acquisition is a massive eye-opener. We need to stop thinking that our only exit path is a massive, multi-million dollar local IPO or a complex acquisition by a local conglomerate. Sometimes, the cleanest exit is building a super-focused, highly-utility tool that some massive global platform needs to complete their ecosystem.

![Street vibe in Nigeria where the hustle never stops](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1550005810-ca9161a0215a?q=80&w=1200&auto=format&fit=crop)

Stakpak’s team is joining Vercel to build the future of AI-powered cloud management. Their open-source projects remain available, meaning the community still benefits from what they started.

So, what is stopping us? Open up your editor. Find that annoying, repetitive thing in your daily deployment workflow that makes you want to throw your laptop out of the window. Write a utility tool to fix it. Open-source it. Put it on GitHub. 

The global tech ecosystem is watching, and they are buying.
